Overview of Base Camps

The most used route to climb K2 Mountain is Abruzzi Spur. Climbers pass through seven base camps on their way to the summit.

K2 base camp

  • Altitude of base camp:5,150 meters (16,896ft)
  • Altitude of K2:8,611 meters (28,251 ft)

Location & Purposes

  • It is situated at base the camp on the Godwin-Austen Glacier
  • It is the headquarters for climbers and teams.
  • This is the starting point for climbers to the summit.

Life at Base Camp

Climbers spend their 3 to 7 days at the base camp. They check their Gear and packing which includes oxygen tanks, ropes, food, and all other essential items for the ascend. They also check the weather conditions to determine the best time for summit.

The first successful ascent by the Italians started from here in 1954.

Resources

  • Tents.
  • Dining area & communication centers.
  • Medical tents.

Advance Base Camp

Altitude:5,600 meter (18,373 ft)

This camp is used as a resting area before moving to Camp 1. It is also used for refilling supplies. There are no permanent tents.

Camp 1: The real climb begins

Altitude:6,100 meters (20,013 ft)

Location & Conditions

  • It is on a Steep, icy slope with deep ice crevasses all around.
  • It takes 6 to 8 hours to climb from the base camp to camp 1.

Challenges & Danger

  • Climbers must cross vertical rock sections called the "House's Chimney".
  • Rockfalls are common.
  • Some climbers experience altitude sickness at this camp.

Camp 2: The Windy Zone

Altitude:6700 meters (21981 ft)

Location & Conditions

  • It is the windiest part of K2
  • Climbers must use ropes to prevent themselves from falling off the ridge.

Challenges & Danger

  • The temperature can drop to -30°C
  • Space is limited, so few tents can be pitched.

Camp 3: The Ice Wall Challenge

Altitude:7,200 meter (23622 ft)

Location & Conditions

  • It is located on steep, icy slopes making it difficult to set up tents.
  • The oxygen level decreases significantly.

Challenges & Dangers

  • Climbers must pass through vertical rock face called the "Black Pyramid".
  • During the 2008 disaster, eleven people was killed by a massive ice collapse.

What Happens Here?

Here climbers sleep in their suits because it is too cold outside. Using oxygen is essential to move onward from this point.

Camp 4: The Death Zone

Altitude:7,800 meter (25,591 ft)

Challenge & Dangers

  • Because of Weakness, frostbite, and sickness most climbers do not survive this part of K2.
  • Here, there is a narrow icy path called Bottleneck where huge chunks of ice can collapse at any time.
  • In 2021, Ali Sadpara a famous Pakistani climber, went missing with two other climbers near camp 4.

Location & Condition

  • It is located at the most dangerous section of K2 called Bottleneck Couloir
  • Here, only one-third of oxygen is available at sea level.
  • The extreme cold temperature drops to -40°C.

What happens here?

Climbers can rest for few hours, then start their summit again at mid night. Also, they try to carry minimal gear to move as fast as possible.

The Summit: Final Victory

Altitude:8,611 meters (28,251 ft)

Altitude & Conditions

  • At final stretches climbers must pass rock faces and ice walls.
  • The summit temperature ranges from -50°C to -60°
  • Many climbers die due to a lack of oxygen.

What Happens Here?

Climbers take photos and celebrate their summit. However, they cannot stay here longer due to extreme weather conditions, they can remain at summit only for 10 to 15 minutes only. They must start descending immediately to avoid running out of oxygen.
