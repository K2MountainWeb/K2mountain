k2: The Deadliest Mountain


K2 is known as the deadliest mountain because it is difficult to climb due to its harsh weather, dangerous slopes.

Conditions

Harsh Weather:

  • The weather suddenly changes while climbing k2, making it dangerous to climb.
  • Temperature level suddenly decreases to -60Â°C.
  • Strong air pressure can blow climber out off the mountain.

Steep slopes:

  • K2 is steeper than Everest, which makes it more dangerous than Everest.
  • There is no chance of survival for a climber they fall.
  • For climbing vertical sections, ropes are essential.

Lack of Oxygen:

  • As climber moves towards peak, oxygen level decreases.
  • If oxygen level decreases, then the climber may die.
  • Climber should use supplementary oxygen to survive at high altitudes.

Rockfalls:

  • In case of rockfalls, the climber may be buried under snow.
  • Falling rocks may hit climbers, which is a major cause of losing balance and fall to their deaths.

Story of Survivors: Conquering K2

Here is the story of some famous climber who climbed the second-highest peak on Earth.

The First Climber of K2:

On July 31st ,1954, two climbers named Achille Compagnoni and Lino Lacedelli, made the first successful ascent on K2. To survive the extreme altitude, they used bottled oxygen. This was a historic achievement.

Images of First climbers

Other successful Survivors

Peter Hillary

In 1995, tow climbers, named Peter Hillary and Alison Hargreaves, tried to climb K2. However, due to deadly storm, Alison died, but Peter Hillary survived.

Grey Mortenson

In 1993, he tried to climb k2, but due to sickness, he couldn't climb it and was saved by a local villager.

Carlos Soria

He was a Spanish climber. He tried five times to climb K2 and survive every time luckily.

  • 1973: He tried to climb but returned due to harsh climate.
  • 1986: 13 climbers were killed in deadly avalanches. But he survived.
  • 1994: He reached at 8000 meter but return due to bad weather.
  • 2004 and 2014 he tried again but still couldn't climb K2.

Tragic Deaths on K2

The Death rate on K2 is 25% for those who reach the summit.

The 2008 k2 disaster

In 2008, eleven climbers died in same day because they got stuck in the "Death Zone" which is 8000 meters above sea level. Huge ice blocks destroyed their ropes causing some of them fall and die, while others got stuck and frozen in ice.

Other Famous accidents:

  • 1986: 13 climbers died due to a sudden flow of ice.
  • 1995: Alison Hargreaves, attempted to climb the k2 solo without supplementary oxygen but died in storm.
  • 2021: Mohammad Ali Sadpara, John snorri and Juan Pablo Mohr attempted a winter ascent of K2. But they went missing and on February 5th ,2021, they were declared dead.
